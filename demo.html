A directed graph can be used to represent an adversarial argument framework where nodes are arguments and edges are attacks between arguments. This demo allows you to navigate through the distinct directed graphs with up to three nodes.

Ground extension

Ground semantics are the most sceptical. Nodes/arguments are in the ground extension if they are unattacked or defended by an argument that is also in the ground extension. For any network there is only ever one ground extension. In this network yellow nodes represent accepted arguments, found in the ground extension. Red nodes are unaccepted arguments, not found in the extension.
